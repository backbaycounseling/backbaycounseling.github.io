Purpose

Coming in May! This group is for new parents with a baby 3 months old or younger to help you connect with other new parents in the area and share the joys and struggles of taking care of a baby. This group can be particularly helpful for those concerned about postpartum complications such as anxiety, depression, and those with traumatic birth experiences. But even if these are not issues you face the group can help you feel supported and connected with others who are also sleep-deprived, stressed, and learning how to care for a newborn. ALL, LGBTQI+, are welcome to this group!

Topics

  • Postpartum anxiety & mood issues
  • Caring for self & newborn
  • Adjusting to parenthood and identity changes
  • Relationship changes & co-parenting
  • coping with sleep-deprivation, decision-making, and societal pressures

Groups

"The problems we feel most alone with are, by definition, a shared reality with millions of others, separated by shame and shyness."

-- Alain de Botton

Group therapy is a powerful treatment modality which can help you feel connected, validate your feelings, and normalize your struggles while also learning together with others strategies for coping with challenges. With groups you can draw on the wisdom of others, create deep bonds, and share your experiences in a safe environment built out of mutual support and compassion. Group therapy is particularly effective for clients who feel isolated which is why Rachel is passionate about providing group support to new parents who often can feel alone, anxious, or depressed during the intense postpartum period. She is equally passionate about connecting victims & survivors of harassment and abuse in the work place as this too can lead to a sense of shame, guilt, or aloneness that groups can help challenge.
